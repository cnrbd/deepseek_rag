3. Maradona's "Hand of God" Goal

One of the most infamous moments in soccer history occurred during the 1986 FIFA World Cup quarter-final between Argentina and England. Argentine captain Diego Maradona scored a controversial goal using his left hand, an action unseen by the referees, leading to widespread debate and earning the moniker "Hand of God" . Minutes later, Maradona showcased his extraordinary skill by dribbling past multiple English players to score what was later voted the "Goal of the Century" . These contrasting goals encapsulate the duality of Maradona's legacy, blending genius with controversy.
